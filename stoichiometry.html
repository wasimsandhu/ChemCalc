What is stoichiometry?

Stoichiometry is a useful way to calculate the theoretical amounts of reactants needed and products created in a chemical reaction. You can find stoichiometric ratios based on the coefficients of a balanced chemical reaction.

N2 + 3H2 → 2NH3

For example, in the above reaction, 1 mole of N2 (nitrogen gas) and 3 moles of H2 (hydrogen gas) are needed to produce 2 moles of ammonia, or NH3.

Remember, a mole is just a number! You could have a dozen eggs (12 eggs), a dozen cars (12 cars) or a dozen dollars ($12). In the same way, you can have a mole of eggs (6.02•1024 eggs), a mole of cars (6.02•1024 cars) and one mole dollars (6.02•1024 dollars)!

How do I find the limiting reactant?

A limiting reactant can be thought of as the reactant that runs out first in a reaction. A reaction can only proceed as long as there are reactants. When one of the reactants runs out, the reaction stops.

To find out which reactant runs out first, we have to use dimensional analysis to "convert" how much of the other reactant is needed. Typically, the conversion goes like this: grams of first reactant → moles of first reactant → moles of second reactant → grams of second reactant.

Let's say we react 10.00 grams of nitrogen with 5.00 grams of hydrogen. Which reactant is the limiting reactant? Don't be tricked into thinking hydrogen because it is the least amount!

How do I find theoretical yield?

Theoretical yield is the theoretical amount of product formed in a reaction. We say theoretical because the actual yield of most reactions will be less.

To find theoretical yield, you first need to find out which reactant is the limiting reactant. Because the reaction stops when the first reactant runs out, no more product is formed. Therefore we must use the amount of limiting reactant in our calculation.

We find theoretical yield using dimensional analysis, similar to how we find limiting reactants (abbreviated as LR from now on). The typical process is as follows: convert grams of LR → moles of LR → moles of product → grams of product.
